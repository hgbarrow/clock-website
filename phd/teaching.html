EE 40 - Spring 2015

Intro to Microelectronic Circuits

In this course, students learned to analyze, design, build and test electronic circuits, and understand their capabilities and limitations. A wide range of topics were covered including:

  • Kirchoff's laws
  • Energy Storage (capacitors and inductors)
  • Time and frequency domain signal representations
  • Power/accuracy/speed tradeoff
  • Laboratory practices (breadboarding, test equipment)

By the end of the semester, each student had amassed enough knowledge to build their own "bouncy-bot." This included programing a MSP430 microcontroller and building several analog circuits.

EE 143 - Spring 2014

Microfabrication Technology

EE143 teaches the fundamentals of integrated-circuit (IC) fabrication and surfacemicromachining technology, giving the student a basic understanding of IC and micromachining processes and the effect of processing choices on device performance.

In the laboratory portion of the course, students were provided hands-on experience fabricating and characterizing a NMOS chip with simple MEMS components.

Students fabricate their own transistors in an instructional cleanroom environment.
